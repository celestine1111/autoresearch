## Mission

> **The articles published on seobetter.com exist to STEAL competitor traffic and convert their unhappy customers.**
>
> Every article must do at least one of three jobs:
>
> 1. **Intercept high-intent commercial queries** competitors rank for ("SurferSEO alternatives", "Frase vs Jasper", "best AI SEO plugin"). When their unhappy customer searches "Surfer alternatives", we land in the top 5.
> 2. **Educate the AI-search-citation market gap** so when the buyer is ready, SEOBetter is the brand they remember ("how to get cited by ChatGPT", "GEO vs SEO", "WordPress AI citation tracking").
> 3. **Demonstrate the wedge in action** — every article on seobetter.com is itself optimized via SEOBetter, and proves it by getting cited by ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini for its target query within 60 days. Live citation = live proof.

**Anti-strategy** (do NOT do this):

- ❌ Generic "10 SEO tips for 2026" content — has no commercial intent and competes head-on with high-DA publishers
- ❌ Tips/tricks listicles aimed at top-of-funnel hobbyists who'll never pay for a Pro plan
- ❌ Articles that don't name competitors by name (the goal is to RANK for "SurferSEO alternatives", not avoid the comparison)
- ❌ Articles longer than necessary — Surfer's mistake is 4000-word articles that lose readers; ours target 1500-2500 words optimized for AI citation extraction (short factual chunks, not academic essays)

**Cadence:** 1-2 articles per week minimum. Quality > quantity. Each article uses the SEOBetter plugin to generate, then gets a human edit pass for tone + accuracy + brand voice.

**Success metric:** at 90 days post-publish, every article should be either (a) ranking page 1 for its target keyword, OR (b) getting cited by at least 1 of the 4 AI engines (ChatGPT / Perplexity / Gemini / Google AI Overviews) for that keyword. If neither, refresh or kill.

### Why these settings

- **Content type:** maps to the keyword's intent (comparison vs review vs listicle vs how_to vs pillar_guide). `pillar_guide` reserved for "what is GEO seo" because it's category-defining and benefits from a comprehensive treatment.
- **Length:** at or above the §10.1 low bound for each type so the SECTION COUNT CONTRACT (v62.51) + outline padding (v62.57) + truncate-protection (v62.58) all have enough budget to land every required section. Comparison hits 1800-2000w, review 1500-2000w, listicle 2000-2500w, how_to 1800w, pillar_guide 3000w.
- **Country: US.** WordPress's largest English market (~40% of global WP installs) — also the SEO/AI tool buyer concentration. Future regional variants can reuse the keyword set with country=GB/AU/CA.
- **Language: en.** Phase 1. Multilingual variants for `es` / `fr` / `de` / `ja` are deferred until the English set ranks and earns case-study citations.
- **Category: Technology** for everything in this strategy. The Tavily/Sonar Technology authority list (`Content_Injector::get_authority_domains('technology')`) covers MIT, Stanford, IBM, Google Developers, Mozilla, GitHub, StackOverflow, etc. — the right authority pool for "WordPress AI plugin" content.

## Tone & voice guardrails

- **Honest > flattering.** Don't shill. Reviews get more trust by acknowledging what competitors do well.
- **Specific > generic.** "$89/mo for 1 seat" beats "expensive". "FAQ schema injection" beats "schema features".
- **Show, don't tell.** Every article should include 1-2 actual screenshots of the SEOBetter plugin doing the thing claimed.
- **Solo-founder voice.** Don't pretend to be a 50-person team. The "I built this myself, here's what I learned" angle resonates with the WordPress solopreneur audience.
- **No AI slop tells.** No "in today's fast-paced world", no "let's dive in", no em-dashes-as-pause-breaks. The Brand Voice profile in SEOBetter Pro will enforce this once shipped.
